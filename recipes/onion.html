French Onion Soup

French Onion Soup is a soul-warming delight. Caramelized onions in a rich beef broth, topped with melted cheese and a toasted baguette, create a harmonious blend of savory flavors and comforting textures.

Ingredients:

  • 4 large onions, thinly sliced
  • 3 tablespoons unsalted butter
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 4 cups beef broth
  • 2 cups chicken broth
  • 1/2 cup dry white or red wine (optional)
  • 1 teaspoon Worcestershire sauce
  • 1 bay leaf
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • Baguette slices, toasted
  • Gruyère or Swiss cheese, grated

Recipe:

  1. Heat the butter and olive oil in a large pot or Dutch oven over medium heat.
  2. Add the sliced onions and cook slowly, stirring occasionally, until they caramelize and turn a deep golden brown color, around 30-40 minutes.
  3. Once the onions are caramelized, pour in the wine (if using) to deglaze the pot, scraping up any browned bits from the bottom.
  4. Add the beef broth, chicken broth, Worcestershire sauce, bay leaf, salt, and pepper to the pot.
  5. Bring the soup to a simmer, then reduce the heat to low and let it simmer gently for about 20-30 minutes to allow the flavors to meld together.
  6. Taste and adjust seasoning if needed.
  7. Preheat the oven broiler. Ladle the soup into oven-safe bowls placed on a baking sheet.
  8. Top each bowl of soup with a toasted baguette slice and sprinkle generously with grated Gruyère or Swiss cheese.
  9. Place the baking sheet with the soup bowls under the broiler for 2-3 minutes, or until the cheese is melted and bubbly, watching carefully to prevent burning.
  10. Remove the soup bowls from the oven and allow the soup to cool for a minute before serving.
  11. Serve hot and enjoy the comforting goodness of homemade French Onion Soup!
